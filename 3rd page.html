About the City of Westminster

The City of Westminster is one of the most historically significant and politically influential areas in the United Kingdom. Located in the heart of London, it is home to many of the nation’s most recognisable landmarks, including the Palace of Westminster, Buckingham Palace, and Westminster Abbey. The borough plays a central role in the cultural, governmental, and ceremonial life of the country.

History and Development

Westminster’s origins date back over a thousand years, with its early development centred around the construction of Westminster Abbey in the 10th century. Over time, the area became the seat of English—and later British—government. The modern borough was officially formed in 1965 through the merger of the Metropolitan Boroughs of Westminster, Paddington, and St Marylebone.

Today, Westminster blends historic architecture with modern urban life. Its streets reflect centuries of political evolution, cultural expression, and architectural innovation.

Government and Civic Life

Westminster City Council oversees local services, planning, and community initiatives. The borough is unique in its combination of residential neighbourhoods, global tourist destinations, and national institutions. It is also home to the UK Parliament, making it the symbolic centre of British democracy.

Culture and Community

Westminster is a diverse and vibrant borough, welcoming millions of visitors each year. From world‑class museums and theatres to historic squares and parks, the area offers a rich cultural landscape. Local communities contribute to a dynamic social fabric, with traditions and modern influences coexisting throughout the borough.

British Values in Westminster

As a borough deeply connected to the nation’s identity, Westminster embodies key British values such as democracy, the rule of law, individual liberty, and mutual respect. These principles are reflected in its institutions, public spaces, and community programmes.
